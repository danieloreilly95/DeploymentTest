2013 University Statistics

The above chart represents the breakdown of Universities in 2013. The chart is broken up between the number of entrants in each individual University in Ireland. The total number of New Entrants to Universities in 2013 was 20.75K with UCD taking 20.43% of those new entrants.



2008 - 2016 Undergrad University Entrants

The above graph represents the number of New Entrants to Universities from 2008 - 2016. The graph shows that there has a been a steady increase in the number of Entrants to Universities, with each year ranging between above 19.5K and less than 21K, until there was a sharp increase in 2015 and 2016. In 2008 the number of entrants to Universities was 19.76K compared to 23.03K in 2016.

Trinity College Dublin Undergrad Entrants

The above graph shows the number of New Entrants in Trinity College Dublin (TCD) from 2008 - 2016. Located in Dublin City Centre, Trinity is Ireland’s highest ranked university and one of the world’s top 100. It has up to 17,000 registered undergraduate and postgraduate students across all major disciplines in the arts and humanities, business, law, engineering, science, and health sciences. University College Dublin Undergrad Entrants

The above graph shows the number of New Entrants in University College Dublin (UCD) from 2008 - 2016. UCD is one of Europe's leading research-intensive universitie. An environment where undergraduate education, masters and PhD training, research, innovation and community engagement form a dynamic spectrum of activity. The international standing of UCD has grown in recent years; it is currently ranked within the top 1% of institutions world-wide. See UCDs website for more information on University College Dublin.

University College Cork Undergrad Entrants

The above graph shows the number of New Entrants in University College Cork (UCC) from 2008 - 2016. University College Cork is a world class university, founded in 1845. UCC is renowned for the quality of its academic programmes, the beauty of its campus and its vibrant student life. The Univeristy is made up of the following four colleges; Arts, Celtic Studies and Social Sciences, Business and Law, Medicine and Health, Science, Engineering and Food Science. See UCCs website for more information on University College Dublin.

Dublin City University Undergrad Entrants

The above graph shows the number of New Entrants in Dublin City University (DCU) from 2008 - 2016. DCU has seen one of the biggest increase of new entrants with just 1.81K new entrants in 2008 compared to 3.45K in 2016. DCU’s academic excellence is recognised and acknowledged around the world. They regularly feature among the top young universities globally and have twice been named the Sunday times University of the year. September 2016 saw the completion of the process by which St Patrick’s College Drumcondra (SPD), Mater Dei Institute (MDI) and Church of Ireland College of Education (CICE) were fully incorporated with DCU expanding the size of the growing University. See DCUs website for more information on Dublin City University.

University of Limerick Undergrad Entrants

The above graph shows the number of New Entrants in the University of Limerick (UL) from 2008 - 2016. The University of Limerick (UL), is located on the west coast of Ireland along the picturesque River Shannon and enjoys an unspoiled natural environment blended with state-of-the-art teaching and research facilities. With close to 12,000 registered students, UL is a young and enterprising university with a proud record of innovation in education and scholarship. Known as ‘Ireland’s Sporting Campus’, UL boasts Ireland’s first altitude training centre, an Olympic-sized swimming pool and the National Strength and Conditioning Centre. See ULs website for more information on the University of Limerick.
