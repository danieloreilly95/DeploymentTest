2015 College Entrants

The above chart represents the breakdown of New Entrants to Colleges in 2015. The chart is broken up between the number of new entrants in each individual College in Ireland. 2014 College Entrants

The above chart represents the breakdown of New Entrants to Colleges in 2014. The chart is broken up between the number of entrants in each individual College in Ireland. Mary Immaculate College took in 35.85% of New Entrants to Colleges in 2014 followed by St Patricks Drumcondra taking 28.43%.

2013 College Entrants

The above chart represents the breakdown of Colleges in 2013. The chart is broken up between the number of entrants in each individual College in Ireland. The total number of New Entrants to Colleges in 2013 was 2.21K with Mary Immaculate College taking 35.85% of those new entrants.



2008 - 2016 Undergraduate College Entrants

The above graph represents the number of New Entrants to Colleges from 2008 - 2016. The graph shows that there has was a steady increase in the number of Entrants to Colleges up until 2014. In 2014 St Patricks Drumcondra and the Mater Dei Institute were absored into Dublin City University and no longer classed as a college insititution. The number of entrants into colleges are generally quite small and there are very few college insitutions in Ireland, in comparison to Institutes of Technology or Universities.

Mary Immaculate College

The above graph shows the number of New Entrants in Mary Immaculate College (MIC) from 2008 - 2016. MIC is a Catholic College of Education and the Liberal Arts. The College offers a wide range of programmes in Education and the Liberal Arts at both undergraduate and postgraduate level and almost 5,000 students are engaged in studies at MIC. See MICs website for more information on Mary Immaculate College.

Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland

The above graph shows the number of New Entrants in the Royal College of Surgeons (RCSI) from 2008 - 2016. RCSI is home to Ireland's largest medical school, one of the worlds leading medical schools as well as Schools of Pharmacy, Physiotherapy, Nursing & Midewifery as well as Postgraduate Studies. RCSI is the professional training body for surgeons in Ireland and is home to Irelands foremost research Institutesand centres. See RCSIs website for more information on the Royal College of Surgeons.

National College of art & Design

The above graph shows the number of New Entrants in the National College of Art & Design (NCAD) from 2008 - 2016. The National College of Art & Design offers a large range of art and design degrees in the state at undergraduate and postgraduate level. The College has 1,000 full time students and a further six hundred students who are taking evening courses. The College has four Schools; Design, Education, Fine Art and Visual Culture. See NCADs website for more information on the National College of Art & Design.

St Angela's College

The above graph shows the number of New Entrants in St Angela's College from 2008 - 2016. St. Angela’s College, Sligo, has a well-deserved reputation for delivering high quality programmes at both undergraduate and postgraduate levels across the academic departments of Nursing and Health Studies, Home Economics and Education. The College offers a wide range of Access, Adult Education and Professional Development programmes. See St Angela's website for more information on St Angelas College, Sligo.
